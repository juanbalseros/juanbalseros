About Me

I am Juan Esteban, a recent civil engineering graduate who was born in Bogotá, Colombia and came to the US to complete my undergraduate studies in engineering. For the last couple of years, I have lived in New York City, which is one of the most ethnically and culturally diverse cities in the world.

I served as a teaching assistant for two upper-level classes: Water Resources Engineering and Fluid Mechanics and Flow Systems. This experience fostered my enthusiasm for both research and teaching. I completed my undergraduate studies at The Cooper Union, where I was fortunate to be surrounded by an incredible group of professors and students.

Research Interests

My research interest centers in using machine learning to improve civil engineering design processes, particularly in optimizing conventional steel and concrete member design. I hope to create practical tools that make these designs more efficient by using machine learning algorithms and large data sets.

Also, I’m interested in the behavior of materials and their response to imposing large deformations, by applying dynamic and non dynamic loading conditions.
